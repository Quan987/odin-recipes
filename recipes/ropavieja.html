Cuban Ropa Vieja Recipe

Description

Transport your taste buds to the vibrant streets of Cuba with our authentic Ropa Vieja recipe, a dish that encapsulates the rich history and flavors of the island nation. Ropa Vieja, meaning "old clothes" in Spanish, is a culinary masterpiece that transforms tender beef into a tapestry of savory indulgence. Slow-cooked to perfection, the beef shreds into thin, succulent strands that mimic the appearance of old, frayed clothing, hence the name. Ingredient:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 pounds beef flank steak
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 (8 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1 (6 ounce) can tomato paste
  • 1 green bell pepper, seeded and sliced into strips
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add flank steak and cook until browned, about 4 minutes per side.
  2. Transfer steak to a slow cooker; pour in beef broth and tomato sauce. Add tomato paste, bell pepper, onion, garlic, cumin, cilantro, olive oil, and vinegar; stir until well blended.
  3. Cover and cook on Low for up to 10 hours, or on High for 4 hours.
  4. Shred steak in the slow cooker with two forks.
